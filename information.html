Topical essay

Algorithms register what we search for, what websites we visit, which items we buy, the videos we watch, the posts we like, etc. This information is often used to provide targeted ads to make us buy something and/or recommendations to make us stay on the site for longer, which might translate into revenue through direct sales or ad space. Algorithms are also used to sort emails, as well as in translation software. These are perhaps some of their most well-known uses. What a lot of people might not be aware of, however, is that algorithms are also used to write certain types of texts, especially the kind that typically follows a certain template, like sports columnsi. However, despite their obvious advantages in a lot of respects, algorithms have also been known to contribute to the spread of right-wing extremism on video sharing platforms and social mediaii. This is not necessarily done intentionally on the developers' part, but comes as a result of how the algorithms themselves work - they want to serve you content that is similar to the kind you've engaged with before. Nevertheless, algorithms are indeed also used in much more deliberate, ill-intentioned ways, which should invoke worry.

Algorithms are used to track your movements, e.g. through datingiii and running appsiv, potentially compromising the security of sexual minorities and military forces. They are also used to record unwitting smartphone usersv. Algorithms are also used in facial recognition software, the kind that is used in smartphones that allow you to unlock the screen by holding the device in front of you. Though that by itself might seem quite innocent, such software can be implemented on a massive scale. In fact, it has been used for several years already. In Moscow, facial recognition software was implemented in public surveillance in 2017, and has led to the arrest of over 1 600 people on public transport in less than a yearvi. Critics have voiced concern regarding how this system might be abused in the future in an increasingly authoritarian Russian state, e.g. by following the most extreme example so far - the social credit system in China.

So even though algorithms can be very useful, the exploitation of them as illustrated above is alarming, and clearly shows that more discussion and regulation around these questions are needed.

Technology and society are deeply connected. Technologies shapes societies and societies affect Technologies. While making technological decisions we need to analyse many its effects deeply.
